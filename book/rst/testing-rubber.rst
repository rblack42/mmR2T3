Testing Rubber
##############

My goal in this effort is to generate a mathematical model of rubber suitable
for use in the design of indoor model airplanes. Why do that?

My training was in **Computational Fluid Dynamics**,(*CFD*), a field basically
focused on using computers to predict how flight vehicles will perform without
needing to rely on extensive testing of actual physical objects (either full
size or models). When I started this training, computers were still mainframe
beasts available only to a few folks. Research in this field was just beginning,
and we used available supercomputers like the Cray-1 to do our work.  Today
this field has advanced to the point that CFD can be used on your personal
computer to help design all kinds of objects that move through fluids of all
kinds.

After I retired, I returned to my favorite hobby, building and flying indoor
rubber powered airplanes. I also decided to revisit my early training in
aerodynamics. The result of all of that was a pair of articles I published in
2021 :cite:`black2021` and 2022 :cite:`black2022`.
